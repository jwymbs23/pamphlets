The popularity of a term is measured as the number of times that it appears in a given year divided by a measure of the number of total documents that the term appears in. This is known as a tf-idf (term frequency - inverse document frequency) scheme, and can help highlight words that appear frequently in some subset of a corpus, but not frequently in the entire corpus, as would common words like "the" and "and".
